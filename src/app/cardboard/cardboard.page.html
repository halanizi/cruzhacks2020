Cardboard

Everything You Need to Know About Cardboard Recycling

What is Cardboard?

Wiki definition: "Cardboard is a generic term for heavy-duty paper-based products having greater thickness and superior durability or other specific mechanical attributes to paper; such as foldability, rigidity and impact resistance". Amazon boxes, pizza boxes, shipping boxes... most boxes are cardboard!

So... what really is recyclable?

Boxes, plates, tubes, paper posters are all recyclable items. Just make sure they're not contaminated with oils or grease! Laminated items such as tape or glossy posters cannot be recycled.

Frequently Asked Questions: (FAQ)

  • How is Cardboard Made?

Cardboard is made from packing fibers together tightly and drying them into sheets. Most cardboard is made from trees, wood, and plant-based materials. Because the materials have many compressed layers, cardboard is found to be much sturdier and thus a good (and inexpensive) packaging option for manufactures. Because cardboard is plant based, it has been responsible for over 50% of all recycled goods!

  • What about pizza boxes?

If the box has grease or oil on it, throw it out! Because cardboard is recycled by mixing it with water, grease and oil won't mix! It is a common misconception that pizza boxes are recyclable.

  • Can the tape or packing foam from boxes be recycled?

Unfortunately, most bubble wrap and packing foam is made from non-recyclable plastics. Check out the plastics section to learn more about all the different types of recyclable plastics!
